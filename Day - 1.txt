1. HTTP1.1:
     HTTP1.1  is developed by Timothy Berners LEE in 1989 as a communication standard for the WWW, HTTP is a top level 
application protocol that exchanges information between a client computer and a local or remote web server.

HTTP/2:
    HTTP/2 began as a SPDY protocol , developed primarily at Google with the intention of reducing web page load latency 
by using techniques such as compression , multiplexing and priotization. This protocol served as a template for HTTP/2 when 
the Hypertext Transfer Protocol working group.

2. Objects and its internal representation:
     Objects can be defined as an unordered collecton of related data of primitive or reference types in the form of 
"key : value" pairs. Objects have an internal property known as Prototype.
